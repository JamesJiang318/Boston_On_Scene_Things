### 2. Shooting Approach (core of autumnal cinematic style)
#### (1) Shot Design (use "seasonal symbols + campus details" to build atmosphere)
- **Establishing shots**:
  - Top-down shot of fallen leaves blowing across the hallway floor (slow motion).
  - Close-up of maple leaves on a classroom windowsill swaying in sunlight (background blurred).
  - Ginkgo branches outside the library window (backlit, with bokeh effects).
- **Character shots**:
  - Close-up of a student resting their cheek on the desk, lit by warm light, twirling a pen (background blurred into warm tones).
  - Medium shot of a group discussion: sunlight slants across the desk, hitting notebooks and a coffee cup.
  - Follow shot of a student carrying books down a leaf-strewn hallway (slow motion for leaves kicking up with their steps).

#### (2) Shooting Settings (for cinematic feel)
- Use a **wide-aperture lens (f/1.8-f/2.8)** to blur the background and highlight the subject.
- Lower shutter speed to **1/50s** (with a stabilizer) for slight motion blur on leaves/hair.
- Set white balance to **5500K + A7 (amber tint)** to capture a warm base tone directly.


### 3. Color Grading Logic (key to enhancing autumnal cinematic style)
Use Premiere Pro/DaVinci Resolve, in 3 steps:
1. **Base correction**:
   - Lower exposure by 10% (darken the frame to emphasize warm light).
   - Increase contrast by 5% (enhance light-dark layers).
2. **Atmospheric grading**:
   - Add **orange-yellow** to shadows (+15) and **warm gold** to highlights (+10).
   - Desaturate "green/cyan" (make plants look yellow-brown) and boost "orange/red" saturation (highlight fallen leaves).
3. **Textural finishing**:
   - Add **subtle film grain** (5-8 intensity).
   - Add a **vignette** (10% strength) to focus on the frame’s center.
